Seesaw Productive Clock

Context: This clock has switch-like behavior where you can toggle between counting the time that you are productive, switching when you want a break on your phone.

  • Context: This clock is mainly for students who have study sessions. This is to see how how much time they actually are productive during their sessions, vs. being on their phone and being distracted from their true tasks.
  • Design decisions: I made sure that the switch were different colors on each side to help differentiate between the toggles. For a UX perspective, I added an option for the user to reset if they wanted to start over more easily. I made the break time red to symbolize that it's not supposed to be what you are doing.
  • Future work: Collect data from multiple resets and sessions to product a summary of your stats for the day or week.

Personal Record Clock

Context: This clock starts at 15 seconds and is meant for short form sprinting. If you beat the 15 seconds, you set a new personal record, and the time resets to that personal record. If you exceed the time, the results at the end tells you how many seconds you were off from beating it.

  • Context: This clock is for runners who want to refine their short sprint runs. For example, 40 yard dash times.
  • Design decisions: I added a progress bar to show visualize how close they are to beating the personal record. I also made sure to specify the time to beat, and the seconds exceeded if you didn't break the record. This is because I know collecting data and documentation is super important to help refine your runs.
  • Future work: Find a way to produce a list of record progression, from times exceeded and times broken.

Water Drank Clock

Context: This clock has multiple layers. It shows a regular analog clock and has a blue ring around it show the quantity of water you should drink at that corresponding time.

  • Context: This clock is for anyone that wants to keep track of their water intake, mostly people watching their health and lifestyle.
  • Design decisions: I made sure that the ring around the clock was blue so that it is intuitive to know that it represented water. I also made the text to show the water quantity was also blue so you can see that it is connected. This is contrasted with the clock that has standard black, white, and red colors to seperate the two measurements (water and time).
  • Future work: There are some formatting issues like the title being overlapped which can be edited. Also making the ring of blue more interactive.
